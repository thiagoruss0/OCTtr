# OCT Retinal Anatomy and Standardized Terminology

## Overview

This document provides the standardized nomenclature for retinal structures as visualized on Spectral-Domain and Swept-Source OCT, based on the International Nomenclature for Optical Coherence Tomography (IN-OCT) Panel consensus from 2014.

## Retinal Layers (Inner to Outer)

### Inner Retinal Layers

| Layer | Abbreviation | OCT Appearance | Clinical Notes |
|-------|--------------|----------------|----------------|
| Internal Limiting Membrane | ILM | Hyperreflective thin line | Interface between retina and vitreous |
| Retinal Nerve Fiber Layer | RNFL | Hyperreflective | Thickest at optic disc, thin at fovea |
| Ganglion Cell Layer | GCL | Hyporeflective | Contains retinal ganglion cell bodies |
| Inner Plexiform Layer | IPL | Hyperreflective | Synapses between bipolar, amacrine, and ganglion cells |
| Inner Nuclear Layer | INL | Hyporeflective | Contains bipolar, horizontal, amacrine cell nuclei |
| Outer Plexiform Layer | OPL | Hyperreflective | Photoreceptor-bipolar cell synapses; Henle fiber layer at fovea |
| Outer Nuclear Layer | ONL | Hyporeflective | Photoreceptor cell nuclei |

### Outer Retinal Bands (Four Hyperreflective Bands)

#### Band 1: External Limiting Membrane (ELM)
- **Old terminology**: Outer limiting membrane (OLM)
- **Structure**: Junctional complexes between Müller cells and photoreceptor inner segments
- **Appearance**: Thin hyperreflective line
- **Clinical significance**:
  - Integrity correlates with visual function
  - Disruption indicates photoreceptor stress or damage
  - Important prognostic marker in DME, RVO, and AMD

#### Band 2: Ellipsoid Zone (EZ)
- **Old terminology**: IS/OS junction, photoreceptor integrity line (PIL)
- **Structure**: Ellipsoid portion of photoreceptor inner segments (packed mitochondria)
- **Appearance**: Prominent hyperreflective band
- **Clinical significance**:
  - Most clinically significant reflectivity marker
  - Disruption correlates directly with visual impairment
  - Key prognostic biomarker across multiple diseases
  - Energy production site (high mitochondrial density)

#### Band 3: Interdigitation Zone (IZ)
- **Old terminology**: Cone Outer Segment Tips (COST), Rod Outer Segment Tips (ROST)
- **Structure**: Contact between photoreceptor outer segments and RPE apical processes
- **Appearance**: Thin hyperreflective line (may be difficult to visualize)
- **Clinical significance**:
  - Reflects photoreceptor-RPE interface integrity
  - Abnormality predicts AMD progression
  - Less consistently visible than EZ

#### Band 4: RPE-Bruch's Membrane Complex (RPE-BM)
- **Old terminology**: RPE/BM, RPE complex
- **Structure**: Retinal pigment epithelium and Bruch's membrane (indistinguishable on SD-OCT)
- **Appearance**: Thick hyperreflective band
- **Clinical significance**:
  - Thicker at fovea
  - Detachments (PEDs) significant in AMD
  - Atrophy visible in geographic atrophy

## Foveal Architecture

### Normal Foveal Features
- **Foveal depression**: Central pit ~350µm diameter
- **Foveal avascular zone (FAZ)**: Capillary-free zone, normally 500-600µm
- **Foveal thickness**: Central subfield typically 200-280µm
- **Layer organization**: Absence of inner retinal layers at foveal center

### Henle Fiber Layer
- Oblique orientation of photoreceptor axons at fovea
- Part of OPL, visible with directional OCT
- Can be misinterpreted as pathological thickening

## Choroidal Structures

### Visualization Requirements
- Enhanced Depth Imaging (EDI-OCT) or Swept-Source OCT
- Normal subfoveal thickness: 190-350µm (varies with age, refraction)

### Choroidal Layers (Outer to Inner)
1. **Suprachoroidea**: Outermost, at scleral interface
2. **Haller's layer**: Large choroidal vessels (outer)
3. **Sattler's layer**: Medium choroidal vessels (middle)
4. **Choriocapillaris**: Innermost capillary layer beneath RPE

### Pachychoroid Features
- Subfoveal thickness >300-350µm
- Dilated outer choroidal vessels (pachyvessels)
- Choriocapillaris attenuation over pachyvessels

## Reflectivity Characteristics

### Hyperreflective Structures
- RNFL, IPL, OPL (synaptic layers)
- ELM, EZ, IZ, RPE-BM (outer bands)
- Hard exudates
- Hemorrhage (variable)
- Fibrosis/scar tissue
- Hyperreflective foci (HRF)

### Hyporeflective Structures
- GCL, INL, ONL (nuclear layers)
- Fluid (SRF, IRF)
- Cysts
- Drusen cores (hyporeflective)
- Photoreceptor loss areas

## Measurement Standards

### Central Subfield Thickness (CST)
- ETDRS central 1mm diameter circle
- Center-involved DME: CST involvement
- Machine-specific normal values (calibration varies)

### Macular Volume
- Typically measured within ETDRS grid
- Useful for monitoring edema resolution

### Choroidal Thickness
- Measured from outer RPE to choroid-scleral interface
- EDI or SS-OCT required for accurate measurement
- Subfoveal measurement most commonly reported

## Anatomical Variants

### Epiretinal Membrane (ERM)
- Hyperreflective line on inner retinal surface
- May cause retinal distortion and thickening

### Vitreomacular Adhesion (VMA)
- Perifoveal vitreous detachment with central attachment
- Diameter of attachment defines VMA vs. VMT

### Vitreomacular Traction (VMT)
- VMA causing anatomical distortion
- Associated with macular thickening or intraretinal changes

## Age-Related Changes

- Decreasing choroidal thickness with age
- Increased drusen prevalence
- RPE changes (migration, atrophy)
- Potential RNFL thinning
